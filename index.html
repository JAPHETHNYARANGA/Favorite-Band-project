The Chainsmokers is an American electronic DJ and production duo consisting of Alexander Pall and Andrew Taggart. They started out by releasing remixes of songs by indie artists. The EDM-pop duo achieved a breakthrough with their 2014 song Selfie, which became a top twenty single in several countries. They have won a Grammy award, two American Music Awards, seven Billboard Music Awards and nine iHeartRadio Music Awards. According to Forbes 2019 list of highest paid celebrities, the Chainsmokers were the highest paid DJs, dethroning Calvin Harris after six years

Alex Pall

Alex Pall was born on May 16, 1985 in New York City, New York, USA as Alexander Pall. He is an actor and composer, known for Words on Bathroom Walls (2020), Paris and Hardflip.

Andrew Taggart

Andrew Taggart (born on December 31, 1989 in Maine) is an American DJ, who makes up 1/2 of The Chainsmokers. After graduating from Syracuse University in 2012, he started the duo with Alex Pall by making music remixes of indie bands. The guys recorded two songs with Priyanka Chopra in 2013 and released their first single, “Selfie,” in Dec. of that year. The song was eventually passed on to Steve Aoki, and they signed to his label, Dim Mak records in 2014.
